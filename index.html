Save Space on the Shelves for Kids Books

E-books for children are on the rise, but the good old dead-tree book bravely persists on kids' bookshelves

by Natasha Madov

duardo Pieta tried reading on an iPad. He liked it well enough, but went back to reading print books for two little reasons: Camilla, 6, and Caterina, 3. He wants to set a good example for his daughters. "They associate the tablet with games and video,” he says. "When they see me on the iPad, they think Im playing. I want them to understand that reading is a serious activity. So I'm buying books again.”
